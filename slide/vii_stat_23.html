Variable Types

  • Qualitative (Non-numerical)
  • Quantitative (Numerical)
  • Discrete
  • Continuous

Discrete vs Continuous

Discrete

Isolated, specific values

  • Natural number, no. of accidents

Continuous

Can take any values between any other values

  • Real number, Height, weight

Examples

  • Gender
  • Religion
  • Temperature
  • Income group (Lower class, Low, Middle, High)
  • Income
  • Distance of stars
  • Radius of screws
  • Diameter of trees
  • Room no.

More Examples

  • Income of a regular employee
  • Income of a freelancer
  • Any unchanging number, e.g, \(\pi\)
  • Result of a die throw
  • Father’s name
  • Mark of a subject
  • GPA of a student

Frequency Distribution

Three things required

  • Range \(R=X_H-X_L+1\)
  • Class Interval (CI)
  • No. of class = \(\frac{R}{CI}\)
  • CI=?
